Semantic vs Non-Semantic HTML Tags

Semantic Tags

Semantic tags are those that clearly describe the meaning in a human and machine-readable way.They help with accesssibility,SEO, and the organization of the document structure.

Section Tags

The <section>tag is used to define section of content that can be grouped together.It's often used for grouping telated content.

Article Tag

The<article>tag is used for self-contained content that could be distributed independently,like a news article or bloh post.

Navigation Tag

The<nav>tag is used for a section of links,usually for navigation menu.

  • Home
  • About Us
  • Services
  • Contact

Header Tag

The<header>tag is used for introductory content or navigation website of the pafe or section.

Welcome to Our Website!

Footer Tag

The<footer>Tag is used to define the footer or links to privacy policies.

© 2025 Example Company.All rights reserved.

Aside Tags

The<aside>tag is used for content that is tangentially related to the content around it,like sidebars or related links.

This is a sidebar with related links.

Non-Semantic Tags

Non-semantic tags do not provide any specific meaning.They are used for grouping elements or styling purposes.

Div Tag

The<div>tag is used for generic grouping of content,with no specific meaning.

This content is inside a ⁢div>element.
