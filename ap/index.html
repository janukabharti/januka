A . P . J ABDUL KALAM

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts.

Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, a small town in Tamil Nadu. Coming from a humble background, he worked hard to excel in his studies and graduated in physics from Saint Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli, and then studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.

He joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Kalam played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 and was affectionately called the "Missile Man of India" for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

He became the 11th President of India in 2002, widely admired for his humility, vision, and dedication to education and youth empowerment. Even after leaving office, he continued to inspire students and wrote numerous books, including Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020.

On 27 July 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, he collapsed and passed away. His life continues to inspire millions across the globe.
